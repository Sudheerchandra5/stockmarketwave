SIP vs Lumpsum: How to Compare Mutual Fund Investing Methods

SIP and lumpsum investing can both work, but they behave differently during volatile markets and require different planning assumptions.

What SIP means

A systematic investment plan invests a fixed amount at regular intervals. It can reduce timing pressure and support disciplined long-term investing.

What lumpsum means

A lumpsum investment deploys a large amount at once. It can benefit more when markets rise soon after investing, but it also has higher timing risk.

How to compare them

Compare total invested amount, expected return, time period, volatility comfort, and your cash-flow situation. Use calculators to test multiple scenarios before deciding.
