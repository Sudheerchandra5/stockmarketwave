When to use this calculator

Use this calculator to understand how an ETF expense ratio can reduce long-term returns. Small annual costs can become meaningful over long holding periods.

How to interpret the result

The expense impact compares growth before and after expenses. A lower expense ratio may improve long-term value if tracking quality and liquidity are similar.

Common mistakes to avoid

Do not choose an ETF based only on expense ratio. Also check tracking error, liquidity, bid-ask spread, assets under management, and index methodology.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is the ETF Expense Impact Calculator used for?

The ETF Expense Impact Calculator is a free etfs calculator that works out value before expenses from investment amount, expected gross return (%), expense ratio (%), and years. Enter your own figures to get an instant result with the formula shown on the page.

What formula does the ETF Expense Impact Calculator use?

It calculates Net return = Gross return - Expense ratio and Expense impact = Gross future value - Net future value. The tool reports value before expenses, value after expenses, expense impact, and net annual return.

Where do I get the numbers for the ETF Expense Impact Calculator?

Take investment amount, expected gross return (%), expense ratio (%), and years from your ETF provider page or exchange. The ETF Expense Impact Calculator never fetches live market data, so the output depends only on the values you type in.

Are the ETF Expense Impact Calculator results financial advice?

No. The ETF Expense Impact Calculator is an educational tool. Treat value before expenses as an estimate, re-check your inputs, and confirm important investment decisions with a qualified professional.

Can I use the ETF Expense Impact Calculator with any currency or market?

Yes. Keep investment amount, expected gross return (%), expense ratio (%), and years in the same currency and consistent units, and the ETF Expense Impact Calculator applies the same calculation to any market.
